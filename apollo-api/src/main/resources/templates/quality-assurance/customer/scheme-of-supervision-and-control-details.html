  • Under this section the following issues shall be included in a brief statement(s)

  • The Scheme of Supervision and Control is a systematic guide to quality management activities. It assists both manufacturer and Product certification body to ascertain that periodic tests and quality assurance activities are being carried out in the process satisfactorily. When the scheme is complied with, it will give an assurance that products are continuously meeting the relevant Kenya Standards or approved specifications.

  • F.2.2.1

    The manufacturer may have a quality manual. In writing up the quality manual, reference may be made to the Guide for Quality Manual for Industry and/or IS0 9001 Quality Management System.

  • F.2.2.2

    All Food manufacturers shall establish and implement a food safety system based on HACCP (Hazard Analysis Critical Control point) EAS 151:2000 to ensure safety and suitability of food for consumption.

  • F.2.2.3

    The manufacturer shall be in possession of and implement the standards including code of practices of the products being produced.

  • The manufacturer shall register for standards levy as per the regulations of the Standards Levy Order, 1990, Legal Notice No. 267 of 22nd June 1990.

  • The Product certification body shall determine the frequency of factory inspections and tests necessary for effective monitoring of the product performance. The manufacturer shall supply samples of the product free of charge whenever required for testing by Product certification body.

  • The manufacturer shall have well equipped testing facilities for inspection/ testing during the whole process of manufacture. Inspection and testing shall be manned by competent and qualified personnel.

  • The manufacturer shall ensure that equipment, instrument gauges and tools used for inspection/testing bear a valid calibration certificate undertaken by KEBS or any other accredited bodies.

  • Quality records in relation to inspection/test data, laboratory analysis, calibration, audit reports, complaints and any other quality control activities shall be maintained by the manufacturer in form of chart or tables etc. for a specified period of time and made available whenever required by the Product certification body.

  • The manufacturer shall ensure that the product is labelled according to the requirements of Kenya Standard(s).

  • If the manufacturer is granted permission to use the mark he/she shall:- i: Pay the fee required ii: Adhere to the scheme of supervision and control iii: Apply the mark on the said products. The Product certification body shall issue a permit to use the standardization mark to a manufacturer for application on product(s) which it is satisfied is/are manufactured in accordance with requirements set out in the Kenya standard or approved specification.

  • F.2.9.1

    The permit shall be issued subject to the provisions of the Standards Act, Cap 496 Laws of Kenya and Permits and Fees Regulations which include: i: The permit-holder shall comply with the general and specific conditions and the contents of the scheme of supervision and control which the Certification Body may issue from time to time. ii: The permit is not transferable. iii: Costs incurred during inspection to determine whether commodities to which standardization has been applied fulfill applicable requirements set forth by the Certification Body shall be defrayed by the permit-holder. iv: The right to use the mark applies only to the commodities listed in the Permit. v: Costs incurred in applying the product certification mark shall be defrayed by the permit-holder. vi: The Certification body reserves the right to revise the regulations for marking certified products. vii: If the Kenya Standard/approved specifications on which the permit is based is revised, the Permit shall be valid only if the permit-holder agrees to comply with the requirements set forth in the revised Kenya Standard or approved specification. viii: The permit shall be valid for the period shown on the permit unless earlier suspended or revoked. ix: Additionally, the manufacturer shall be required to insert the permit number in the format "SM#PERMIT NO.” in the labels immediately below the Standardization Mark logo. x: The recommended size of the Standardization Mark shall be minimum 20mm high by 12 mm breadth. xi: In the event that for any reason there is stoppage of production with regard to the product described on the permit, the manufacturer shall be obliged to notify the Managing Director of Kenya Bureau of Standards about the stoppage, for the suspension of the permit. Failure to do so shall be deemed as misuse of the permit whether valid or expired and shall lead to demand to pay accrued monies in the period that renewal of same permit had not taken place.

  • F.2.9.2

    The breach of any of the conditions constitute an offence under the provisions of Standards act and the Permits and Fees Regulations.

  • The permit holder shall be responsible for guaranteeing that all products which carry the standardization mark always meet the quality requirements specified in the relevant standard(s). The client shall only make claims regarding certification consistent with the scope of certification. The fact that any product complies or is alleged to comply with a Kenya standard or approved specification or has been or is alleged to have been manufactured in accordance with any such specification, or that a standardization mark is used in connection with any product, shall not give rise to any claim against the Government, the National Standards Council and the Product certification body, or any member or employee thereof.

  • F2.11.1

    When the certification scheme introduces new or revised requirements that affect the client the Product certification body shall communicate the changes to the client. The Product certification body shall verify the implementation of the changes by the client during surveillance.

  • F2.11.2

    The permit holder shall inform the PCB without delay of any changes including and not limited to major changes in scope of certification, product formulation, relocation of production site, changes in organizational status, changes in ownership or stoppage of production among others. The PCB shall evaluate whether the changes will affect the certification.

  • F2.11.3

    The PCB will evaluate, review and make decision of certification, extend or reduce the scope of certification and revise surveillance activities based on the changes.

  • F2.12.1

    The manufacturer shall suspend application of the Standardization Mark on the product whenever directed by the Product certification body either for failure to conform to the requirements of the standard or any other reason. Marking shall resume only after the Product certification body gives permission to do so.

  • F2.12.2

    In the case of suspension or withdrawal of a permit to use the standardization mark, the onus of removing the mark from all the products previously certified rests with the permit holder and failure to do so shall lead to legal action being taken against the permit holder.

  • F2.12.3

    Upon suspension, withdrawal, or termination of certification, the manufacturer shall return all the certification documents including the original permits.

  • F2.12.4

    Upon expiry of the permit, the manufacturer must discontinue use of the standardization mark.

  • F2.12.5

    Product Recall -The manufacturer shall develop and document a procedure for product recall/ withdrawal.

  • Manufacturer shall maintain a customer complaint register. The register shall contain information on investigation and corrective actions taken which shall be available to the Product certification body on request

  • Under this section the following issues shall be covered: Process flow chart showing operations, inspection and testing points, standard requirements test method(s) sampling procedures, number of Samples, lot size, frequency of inspection/testing evaluation of test results, evaluation of competence of staff of factory, housekeeping & hygiene, environmental consideration, product recall, handling of non-complying products and rejects, evaluation of measuring and testing equipment including calibration, discuss and agree on targets of implementation and any other remarks shall be available.
